# Drug-Enzyme Interaction Database

## Fluconazole Enzyme Interactions
- Drug: Fluconazole
- Primary enzyme interaction: CYP2C9 inhibitor (strong)
- Secondary enzyme interaction: CYP3A4 inhibitor (moderate)
- Tertiary enzyme interaction: CYP2C19 inhibitor (moderate)
- Mechanism of CYP2C9 inhibition: Azole nitrogen coordinates with iron in CYP2C9 active site, blocking substrate access
- Reversibility: Reversible inhibition; effect resolves when fluconazole cleared (half-life 30 hours, takes 5 to 6 days for full clearance)
- Onset of inhibition: Begins with first dose, maximum effect by day 4 to 5
- CYP2C9 substrate drugs affected: Warfarin (S-warfarin), Phenytoin, Glipizide, Glyburide, Tolbutamide, Losartan, Celecoxib
- CYP3A4 substrate drugs affected: Atorvastatin, Simvastatin, Amlodipine, Cyclosporine, Tacrolimus, Sildenafil
- Degree of warfarin INR increase expected: INR typically increases 50 to 100 percent with fluconazole 200mg; may double or triple at higher doses
- Degree of sulfonylurea level increase: Blood glucose-lowering effect increases 50 to 80 percent
- Degree of tacrolimus level increase: Tacrolimus levels may increase 2 to 4 fold
- Degree of cyclosporine level increase: Cyclosporine levels may increase 50 to 100 percent

## Warfarin Enzyme Interactions
- Drug: Warfarin (specifically S-warfarin enantiomer, more potent)
- Primary metabolism: CYP2C9 (S-warfarin)
- Secondary metabolism: CYP3A4, CYP1A2 (R-warfarin, less potent)
- Effect of CYP2C9 inhibition: Reduced warfarin clearance, warfarin accumulates, anticoagulation increases, bleeding risk rises
- Effect of CYP2C9 induction: Increased warfarin clearance, INR falls, clot risk rises
- CYP2C9 inhibitors increasing warfarin effect: Fluconazole, Amiodarone, Metronidazole, Miconazole, Sulfonamides
- CYP2C9 inducers decreasing warfarin effect: Rifampin, Carbamazepine, Phenytoin
- Genetic impact: CYP2C9*2 and *3 carriers need lower warfarin doses; increased bleeding risk
- Protein binding displacement: Drugs displacing warfarin from albumin transiently increase free warfarin

## Simvastatin Enzyme Interactions
- Drug: Simvastatin
- Primary metabolism: CYP3A4 (extensive)
- Effect of CYP3A4 inhibition: Simvastatin accumulates in bloodstream and muscle
- Clinical consequence of accumulation: Myopathy, rhabdomyolysis (muscle breakdown releasing myoglobin into blood)
- Rhabdomyolysis cascade: Myoglobin filtered by kidneys causes acute tubular necrosis and acute kidney injury
- Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors contraindicated with simvastatin: Ketoconazole, Itraconazole, Clarithromycin, Telithromycin, Cyclosporine, Nefazodone
- Moderate CYP3A4 inhibitors requiring dose limit: Amiodarone (simvastatin max 20mg), Amlodipine (simvastatin max 20mg), Verapamil (simvastatin max 10mg), Diltiazem (simvastatin max 10mg)
- Fluconazole interaction with simvastatin: Moderate CYP3A4 inhibition increases simvastatin levels; monitor for muscle symptoms
- FDA action: FDA issued safety communication 2011 restricting simvastatin 80mg dose due to rhabdomyolysis risk

## Atorvastatin Enzyme Interactions
- Drug: Atorvastatin
- Primary metabolism: CYP3A4 (moderate)
- Comparison to simvastatin: Less dependent on CYP3A4 than simvastatin; lower interaction risk
- Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors: Increase atorvastatin levels 2 to 3 fold; increase myopathy risk
- Cyclosporine interaction: Increases atorvastatin AUC 10-fold; maximum atorvastatin dose 10mg with cyclosporine
- Clarithromycin interaction: Increases atorvastatin AUC 4.5-fold; temporary suspension or dose reduction recommended
- Fluconazole interaction with atorvastatin: Moderate; monitor for muscle symptoms with concurrent use

## Tacrolimus Enzyme Interactions
- Drug: Tacrolimus
- Primary metabolism: CYP3A4 (extensive)
- Also substrate of: P-glycoprotein (P-gp)
- Effect of CYP3A4 inhibition: Tacrolimus levels rise rapidly and dramatically
- Effect of CYP3A4 induction: Tacrolimus levels fall, rejection risk increases
- Narrow therapeutic index: Small changes in level cause toxicity or rejection
- Fluconazole effect on tacrolimus: CYP3A4 inhibition increases tacrolimus trough 2 to 4 fold within 2 to 5 days
- Clinical management: Reduce tacrolimus dose by 30 to 50 percent when starting fluconazole; monitor levels daily
- Rifampin effect: CYP3A4 induction drops tacrolimus to subtherapeutic levels within days; may require 3 to 5 fold dose increase
- Clarithromycin effect: CYP3A4 inhibition similar to fluconazole; levels may triple

## Clopidogrel Enzyme Interactions
- Drug: Clopidogrel
- Metabolism note: Prodrug requiring activation
- Activation enzyme: CYP2C19 (primary), CYP3A4 (secondary)
- Effect of CYP2C19 inhibition: Reduced clopidogrel activation, reduced antiplatelet effect, increased clot risk
- Clinical consequence: Stent thrombosis risk in patients with coronary stents taking CYP2C19 inhibitors
- Drugs inhibiting CYP2C19 (reducing clopidogrel effect): Omeprazole, Esomeprazole, Fluoxetine, Fluvoxamine
- Omeprazole interaction significance: FDA issued advisory 2009 recommending against routine omeprazole use with clopidogrel
- Genetic poor metabolizers: CYP2C19*2 and *3 carriers have reduced clopidogrel benefit; FDA black box warning
- Fluconazole effect: Moderate CYP2C19 inhibition; may reduce clopidogrel activation in susceptible patients

## Metformin Transport Interactions
- Drug: Metformin
- Metabolism: None; not metabolized by CYP enzymes
- Elimination: Renal excretion via OCT2 (uptake) and MATE1/MATE2-K (efflux)
- Effect of OCT2 inhibition: Reduces metformin renal secretion, increases plasma metformin levels
- Effect of MATE inhibition: Same outcome; plasma metformin accumulates
- Clinical consequence of accumulation: Increased lactic acidosis risk (especially in renal impairment)
- Drugs inhibiting OCT2 and MATE: Cimetidine, Trimethoprim, Pyrimethamine, Vandetanib, Dofetilide
- Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors: Topiramate increases metformin levels modestly
- Note: Fluconazole does NOT inhibit OCT2 or MATE; no direct pharmacokinetic interaction between fluconazole and metformin
- Indirect interaction concern: Fluconazole can cause nephrotoxicity at high doses; any renal impairment reduces metformin clearance

## Amiodarone Enzyme Interactions
- Drug: Amiodarone
- Enzyme interactions: Inhibits CYP2C9 (moderate to strong), CYP3A4 (moderate), CYP2D6 (moderate), P-gp
- Long half-life significance: Enzyme inhibition persists for months after amiodarone discontinued
- Warfarin interaction: CYP2C9 inhibition increases warfarin levels; INR may double or triple; warfarin dose reduction 30 to 50 percent typically needed
- Digoxin interaction: P-gp inhibition increases digoxin levels; digoxin dose reduction 50 percent recommended
- Simvastatin interaction: CYP3A4 inhibition; simvastatin maximum dose 20mg with amiodarone
- Onset of interaction: Begins within days of starting amiodarone; full effect over weeks
- Duration after stopping: Interaction may persist 3 to 6 months due to long half-life

## Cyclosporine Enzyme Interactions
- Drug: Cyclosporine
- As substrate: Metabolized by CYP3A4 and transported by P-gp
- As inhibitor: Inhibits CYP3A4, P-gp, OATP1B1, OATP1B3
- Statin interaction via OATP1B1: Cyclosporine inhibits hepatic uptake of statins; atorvastatin levels increase 10-fold; simvastatin levels increase dramatically
- Tacrolimus interaction: Additive nephrotoxicity; not usually combined
- Fluconazole effect on cyclosporine: CYP3A4 inhibition increases cyclosporine levels 50 to 100 percent
- Clinical consequence: Cyclosporine nephrotoxicity and neurotoxicity

## Digoxin Transport Interactions
- Drug: Digoxin
- Metabolism: Minimal hepatic metabolism; primarily renal excretion
- Transport: P-glycoprotein substrate (major)
- P-gp inhibitors increasing digoxin: Amiodarone (increases digoxin 70 percent), Verapamil, Quinidine, Clarithromycin, Dronedarone, Cyclosporine
- P-gp inducers decreasing digoxin: Rifampin (reduces digoxin 30 percent)
- Renal interaction: Any drug reducing GFR reduces digoxin clearance; digoxin accumulates
- Electrolyte interactions: Hypokalemia (caused by diuretics) increases digoxin toxicity risk by increasing digoxin binding to Na/K-ATPase
- Spironolactone effect on digoxin assay: Spironolactone metabolites cross-react with digoxin immunoassay; may cause falsely elevated digoxin levels
