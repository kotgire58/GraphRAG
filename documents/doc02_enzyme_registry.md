# Metabolic Enzyme Registry — CYP450 and Related Pathways

## CYP3A4
- Full name: Cytochrome P450 3A4
- Enzyme family: CYP450
- Location: Liver (primary), small intestine
- Percentage of drug metabolism handled: approximately 50 percent of all clinically used drugs
- Function: Oxidative metabolism of xenobiotics and endogenous compounds
- Substrates (drugs metabolized by this enzyme): Atorvastatin, Simvastatin, Amlodipine, Cyclosporine, Tacrolimus, Sildenafil, Clarithromycin, Ketoconazole, Midazolam, Alprazolam, Fentanyl, Carbamazepine
- Known inhibitors (drugs that reduce CYP3A4 activity): Clarithromycin, Ketoconazole, Itraconazole, Fluconazole (moderate), Cyclosporine, Grapefruit juice, Ritonavir, Amiodarone
- Known inducers (drugs that increase CYP3A4 activity): Rifampin, Phenytoin, Carbamazepine, St. John's Wort, Dexamethasone
- Clinical significance: High — inhibition causes substrate drug accumulation; induction causes substrate drug under-exposure
- Genetic variants: CYP3A4*22 associated with reduced enzyme activity

## CYP2C9
- Full name: Cytochrome P450 2C9
- Enzyme family: CYP450
- Location: Liver
- Percentage of drug metabolism handled: approximately 15 percent of clinically used drugs
- Function: Oxidative metabolism, particularly of acidic lipophilic compounds
- Substrates (drugs metabolized by this enzyme): Warfarin (S-warfarin), Phenytoin, Fluconazole, Losartan, Celecoxib, Ibuprofen, Glipizide, Glyburide, Tolbutamide
- Known inhibitors: Fluconazole (strong), Amiodarone (moderate to strong), Metronidazole, Miconazole, Sulfonamides
- Known inducers: Rifampin, Carbamazepine, Phenytoin, Phenobarbital
- Clinical significance: High — warfarin has narrow therapeutic index and is primary substrate
- Genetic variants: CYP2C9*2 and CYP2C9*3 associated with significantly reduced enzyme activity, higher bleeding risk with warfarin

## CYP2C19
- Full name: Cytochrome P450 2C19
- Enzyme family: CYP450
- Location: Liver
- Percentage of drug metabolism handled: approximately 10 percent of clinically used drugs
- Function: Oxidative metabolism
- Substrates (drugs metabolized by this enzyme): Omeprazole, Clopidogrel (activation), Diazepam, Phenytoin, Escitalopram, Voriconazole
- Known inhibitors: Omeprazole, Esomeprazole, Fluvoxamine, Fluconazole, Ticlopidine
- Known inducers: Rifampin, Carbamazepine
- Clinical significance: Very high for clopidogrel — CYP2C19 poor metabolizers cannot activate clopidogrel, lose antiplatelet benefit
- Genetic variants: CYP2C19*2 and CYP2C19*3 (poor metabolizers, 2 to 15 percent of population); CYP2C19*17 (ultra-rapid metabolizers)
- FDA black box warning: Clopidogrel efficacy reduced in poor metabolizers

## CYP2D6
- Full name: Cytochrome P450 2D6
- Enzyme family: CYP450
- Location: Liver, brain
- Percentage of drug metabolism handled: approximately 25 percent of clinically used drugs
- Function: Oxidative metabolism of amines
- Substrates (drugs metabolized by this enzyme): Codeine, Tramadol, Metoprolol, Carvedilol, Haloperidol, Risperidone, Tamoxifen, Atomoxetine, Dextromethorphan
- Known inhibitors: Fluoxetine, Paroxetine, Bupropion, Quinidine, Terbinafine
- Known inducers: Rifampin, Dexamethasone
- Clinical significance: Very high — codeine toxicity in ultra-rapid metabolizers; tamoxifen failure in poor metabolizers
- Genetic variants: Most polymorphic CYP enzyme; poor metabolizers 5 to 10 percent of Caucasians; ultra-rapid metabolizers common in North African populations

## CYP1A2
- Full name: Cytochrome P450 1A2
- Enzyme family: CYP450
- Location: Liver
- Percentage of drug metabolism handled: approximately 9 percent of clinically used drugs
- Function: Oxidative metabolism, activation of procarcinogens
- Substrates (drugs metabolized by this enzyme): Clozapine, Olanzapine, Theophylline, Caffeine, Tacrine, Tizanidine
- Known inhibitors: Fluvoxamine, Ciprofloxacin, Mexiletine, Amiodarone
- Known inducers: Smoking (tobacco), Omeprazole, Rifampin, Cruciferous vegetables
- Clinical significance: Moderate — clozapine toxicity with fluvoxamine co-administration

## P-glycoprotein (P-gp)
- Full name: P-glycoprotein (ABCB1 transporter)
- Classification: Efflux transporter, not CYP enzyme
- Location: Intestinal epithelium, blood-brain barrier, kidney, liver
- Function: Pumps drugs out of cells, reduces absorption and increases excretion
- Substrates: Digoxin, Dabigatran, Cyclosporine, Tacrolimus, Paclitaxel, Loperamide
- Known inhibitors (increase substrate drug levels): Clarithromycin, Ketoconazole, Cyclosporine, Amiodarone, Verapamil, Quinidine
- Known inducers (decrease substrate drug levels): Rifampin, St. John's Wort, Carbamazepine
- Clinical significance: Very high for digoxin — narrow therapeutic index drug heavily affected by P-gp inhibition
- Genetic variants: ABCB1 polymorphisms affect drug absorption and distribution

## OCT2 (Organic Cation Transporter 2)
- Full name: Organic Cation Transporter 2 (SLC22A2)
- Classification: Renal uptake transporter
- Location: Kidney (basolateral membrane of proximal tubule)
- Function: Mediates renal secretion of cationic drugs
- Substrates: Metformin, Creatinine, Cisplatin
- Known inhibitors: Cimetidine, Trimethoprim, Verapamil, Pyrimethamine
- Clinical significance: High for Metformin — OCT2 inhibition reduces metformin renal clearance, increases plasma levels
- Note: Metformin does not undergo hepatic metabolism; OCT2 is primary elimination pathway

## MATE1 and MATE2-K
- Full name: Multidrug and toxin extrusion proteins 1 and 2-K
- Classification: Renal efflux transporters
- Location: Kidney (luminal membrane of proximal tubule)
- Function: Final step in renal secretion of organic cations
- Substrates: Metformin, Creatinine
- Known inhibitors: Cimetidine, Trimethoprim, Pyrimethamine, Vandetanib
- Clinical significance: High for Metformin — MATE inhibition combined with OCT2 inhibition significantly reduces metformin clearance
- Relationship to OCT2: OCT2 handles uptake into tubule cells; MATE handles efflux into urine; both needed for complete metformin secretion

## ACE (Angiotensin Converting Enzyme)
- Full name: Angiotensin Converting Enzyme
- Classification: Peptidyl-dipeptidase enzyme
- Location: Lung (primary), vascular endothelium, kidney
- Function: Converts angiotensin I to angiotensin II; degrades bradykinin
- Inhibited by: Lisinopril, Enalapril, Ramipril, Captopril (all ACE inhibitors)
- Effect of inhibition: Reduces angiotensin II (vasodilation, lower blood pressure), increases bradykinin (side effect: cough)
- Clinical significance: ACE inhibition in diabetic patients with kidney disease slows progression of diabetic nephropathy
- Relevant interaction: ACE inhibitors combined with potassium-sparing diuretics increase hyperkalemia risk

## Aldosterone Synthase (CYP11B2)
- Full name: Aldosterone synthase, Cytochrome P450 11B2
- Classification: Steroidogenic enzyme
- Location: Adrenal cortex (zona glomerulosa)
- Function: Final step in aldosterone biosynthesis
- Relevance to drug interactions: Aldosterone promotes sodium retention and potassium excretion; drugs affecting aldosterone pathway alter electrolyte balance
- Affected by: ACE inhibitors (reduce angiotensin II, reduce aldosterone), ARBs, Spironolactone (aldosterone antagonist)
- Clinical significance: Multiple drugs affecting aldosterone pathway simultaneously increase hyperkalemia risk

## Xanthine Oxidase
- Full name: Xanthine oxidase
- Classification: Oxidoreductase enzyme
- Location: Liver, intestinal mucosa
- Function: Oxidizes hypoxanthine to xanthine to uric acid
- Substrates: Hypoxanthine, xanthine, some purine-based drugs
- Inhibited by: Allopurinol, Febuxostat
- Clinical significance: Allopurinol inhibition of xanthine oxidase affects metabolism of azathioprine and 6-mercaptopurine, causing severe toxicity
- Relevant drug interaction: Azathioprine dose must be reduced by 75 percent when combined with allopurinol

## Vitamin K Epoxide Reductase (VKORC1)
- Full name: Vitamin K epoxide reductase complex subunit 1
- Classification: Reductase enzyme
- Location: Liver
- Function: Recycles vitamin K epoxide back to active vitamin K; required for activation of clotting factors II, VII, IX, X
- Inhibited by: Warfarin
- Effect of inhibition: Clotting factors cannot be activated, anticoagulation occurs
- Clinical significance: Primary target of warfarin anticoagulation therapy
- Genetic variants: VKORC1 polymorphisms significantly affect warfarin dose requirements
- Interaction note: Any drug affecting CYP2C9 (which metabolizes warfarin) will alter warfarin effectiveness
