# Medical Conditions Registry

## Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
- ICD-10 code: E11
- Classification: Metabolic disorder
- Definition: Insulin resistance combined with progressive insulin secretory defect
- Prevalence: 11.3 percent of US adults (2022 CDC data)
- Primary organ affected: Pancreas (insulin secretion), liver (glucose production), muscle (glucose uptake)
- Key biomarker: HbA1c (target below 7 percent for most patients)
- Fasting glucose diagnostic threshold: 126 mg/dL or above on two occasions
- Complications: Diabetic nephropathy, diabetic retinopathy, peripheral neuropathy, cardiovascular disease
- Renal complication detail: Diabetic nephropathy leads to reduced GFR (glomerular filtration rate)
- Kidney function monitoring: Required regularly in all Type 2 Diabetes patients
- Common comorbidities: Hypertension (present in 70 percent of Type 2 Diabetes patients), Dyslipidemia, Obesity, Cardiovascular disease

## Hypertension
- ICD-10 code: I10
- Classification: Cardiovascular disorder
- Definition: Persistent blood pressure at or above 130/80 mmHg
- Prevalence: 47 percent of US adults (2022 AHA data)
- Primary organ affected: Blood vessels, heart, kidneys
- Key biomarker: Systolic and diastolic blood pressure measurements
- Stage 1 threshold: 130 to 139 systolic or 80 to 89 diastolic
- Stage 2 threshold: 140 or higher systolic or 90 or higher diastolic
- Complications: Stroke, myocardial infarction, heart failure, chronic kidney disease, aortic aneurysm
- Renal complication detail: Hypertensive nephrosclerosis leads to reduced GFR over time
- Common comorbidities: Type 2 Diabetes (present in 30 percent of hypertension patients), Dyslipidemia, Obesity, Sleep apnea
- Note: Hypertension and Type 2 Diabetes frequently co-occur; combined presence accelerates kidney damage

## Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
- ICD-10 code: N18
- Classification: Renal disorder
- Definition: Persistent reduction in kidney function for 3 or more months
- Staging: Stage 1 (GFR above 90), Stage 2 (GFR 60 to 89), Stage 3 (GFR 30 to 59), Stage 4 (GFR 15 to 29), Stage 5 (GFR below 15)
- Prevalence: 15 percent of US adults
- Primary cause: Diabetic nephropathy (44 percent of cases), Hypertensive nephrosclerosis (28 percent)
- Key biomarker: GFR (glomerular filtration rate), serum creatinine, urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio
- Drug dosing impact: Many drugs require dose reduction in CKD; renally cleared drugs accumulate
- Critical threshold for Metformin: Contraindicated when GFR falls below 30 mL/min/1.73m2
- Critical threshold for general caution: Dose review required when GFR falls below 60

## Atrial Fibrillation
- ICD-10 code: I48
- Classification: Cardiac arrhythmia
- Definition: Irregular rapid heart rate caused by chaotic electrical activity in atria
- Prevalence: 2 to 3 percent of general population; increases with age
- Primary organ affected: Heart (atria)
- Key biomarker: Electrocardiogram showing irregular rhythm, absent P waves
- Complications: Stroke (5 times higher risk), heart failure, cardiomyopathy
- Stroke prevention: Anticoagulation therapy required (warfarin or direct oral anticoagulants)
- Common comorbidities: Hypertension (most common), Heart failure, Coronary artery disease, Type 2 Diabetes, Sleep apnea

## Heart Failure
- ICD-10 code: I50
- Classification: Cardiovascular disorder
- Definition: Heart cannot pump sufficient blood to meet body demands
- Classification types: HFrEF (reduced ejection fraction below 40 percent), HFpEF (preserved ejection fraction above 50 percent)
- Prevalence: 6.2 million US adults
- Key biomarker: BNP, NT-proBNP, echocardiogram ejection fraction
- Complications: Pulmonary edema, cardiogenic shock, sudden cardiac death
- Common comorbidities: Hypertension, Coronary artery disease, Type 2 Diabetes, Atrial fibrillation, CKD
- Drug accumulation risk: Reduced cardiac output reduces drug distribution and elimination

## Dyslipidemia
- ICD-10 code: E78
- Classification: Metabolic disorder
- Definition: Abnormal levels of lipids in blood (high LDL, low HDL, high triglycerides)
- Prevalence: 38 percent of US adults have high LDL cholesterol
- Key biomarker: LDL cholesterol (target below 70 mg/dL for high-risk patients), HDL, triglycerides
- Complications: Atherosclerosis, cardiovascular disease, pancreatitis (severe hypertriglyceridemia)
- Common comorbidities: Type 2 Diabetes, Hypertension, Obesity, Hypothyroidism
- Statin use: Statins are primary treatment; metabolism via CYP3A4 creates interaction risk

## Fungal Infection (Systemic)
- ICD-10 code: B49 (unspecified)
- Types relevant to hospitalized patients: Candidiasis (Candida species), Aspergillosis, Cryptococcosis
- Common presentation in diabetic patients: Vaginal candidiasis, oral candidiasis, urinary tract candidiasis
- Risk factors: Diabetes mellitus (high glucose promotes fungal growth), immunosuppression, antibiotic use, catheterization
- Diabetes-specific risk: Hyperglycemia impairs neutrophil function, significantly increasing fungal infection risk
- Treatment: Azole antifungals (fluconazole first-line for candidiasis), echinocandins for severe cases
- Treatment duration: 1 to 14 days depending on type and severity

## Hyperkalemia
- ICD-10 code: E87.5
- Classification: Electrolyte disorder
- Definition: Serum potassium above 5.0 mEq/L
- Mild range: 5.0 to 5.5 mEq/L
- Moderate range: 5.5 to 6.0 mEq/L
- Severe range: Above 6.0 mEq/L (medical emergency)
- Symptoms: Muscle weakness, fatigue, palpitations, cardiac arrhythmia, cardiac arrest
- Cardiac risk: Hyperkalemia can cause fatal ventricular fibrillation
- Causes: CKD (reduced potassium excretion), ACE inhibitors, ARBs, potassium-sparing diuretics, NSAIDs, excessive potassium intake
- Drug-induced hyperkalemia risk: Highest when multiple potassium-retaining drugs combined in patients with CKD

## Hypoglycemia
- ICD-10 code: E16.0
- Classification: Metabolic emergency
- Definition: Blood glucose below 70 mg/dL
- Severe definition: Below 54 mg/dL or requiring external assistance
- Symptoms: Sweating, trembling, confusion, loss of consciousness, seizures, death (severe)
- Causes in diabetic patients: Excess insulin, sulfonylureas, missed meals, drug interactions, alcohol
- Drug interaction risk: Fluconazole inhibits CYP2C9 which metabolizes sulfonylureas (glipizide, glyburide), causing drug accumulation and prolonged hypoglycemia
- ACE inhibitor note: Lisinopril may enhance insulin sensitivity, modestly increasing hypoglycemia risk in insulin-treated diabetics
- Treatment: Oral glucose (mild), IV dextrose (severe), glucagon injection

## Lactic Acidosis
- ICD-10 code: E87.2
- Classification: Metabolic emergency
- Definition: Blood lactate above 5 mmol/L with pH below 7.35
- Mortality rate: 50 percent without prompt treatment
- Metformin-associated lactic acidosis (MALA): Rare but serious complication of metformin therapy
- MALA mechanism: Metformin inhibits mitochondrial complex I, increases lactate production; normally cleared by kidneys; accumulates in renal impairment
- MALA incidence: Approximately 3 cases per 100,000 patient-years
- MALA risk factors: Renal impairment, hepatic impairment, dehydration, contrast dye administration, alcohol excess, heart failure
- Contrast dye interaction: IV contrast causes temporary renal impairment; metformin must be held before and after contrast procedures
- Symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, respiratory distress, altered consciousness

## Coronary Artery Disease
- ICD-10 code: I25
- Classification: Cardiovascular disorder
- Definition: Atherosclerotic narrowing of coronary arteries
- Prevalence: 18.2 million US adults
- Key biomarker: Troponin (acute), stress test, coronary angiography
- Complications: Angina, myocardial infarction, sudden cardiac death
- Common comorbidities: Hypertension, Dyslipidemia, Type 2 Diabetes, Atrial fibrillation
- Antiplatelet therapy: Aspirin and/or clopidogrel often prescribed; clopidogrel activation requires CYP2C19

## Organ Transplant (Post-transplant status)
- ICD-10 code: Z94 (various)
- Types: Kidney transplant, liver transplant, heart transplant, lung transplant
- Immunosuppression requirement: Lifelong to prevent rejection
- Primary immunosuppressants: Tacrolimus, Cyclosporine, Mycophenolate, Sirolimus
- Critical drug interaction risk: Tacrolimus and Cyclosporine are narrow therapeutic index drugs metabolized by CYP3A4; any CYP3A4 inhibitor causes dangerous accumulation
- Fluconazole interaction: Fluconazole inhibits CYP3A4, dramatically increases tacrolimus and cyclosporine levels, causes nephrotoxicity and neurotoxicity
- Monitoring requirement: Drug levels must be monitored weekly or more often when starting new medications
