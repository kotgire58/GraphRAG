# Contraindications and Drug Safety Alerts

## Fluconazole + Warfarin
- Interaction ID: DDI-001
- Drug A: Fluconazole
- Drug B: Warfarin
- Severity: Major
- Mechanism: Fluconazole inhibits CYP2C9, which is the primary enzyme metabolizing S-warfarin (the more potent enantiomer)
- Clinical consequence: Warfarin accumulates, INR rises significantly, bleeding risk increases
- Expected INR change: INR typically increases 50 to 100 percent; may exceed therapeutic range into dangerous territory
- Timeframe: INR begins rising within 1 to 2 days of fluconazole initiation
- Management: Do not avoid combination but requires mandatory INR monitoring within 2 to 3 days of starting fluconazole; anticipate warfarin dose reduction of 25 to 50 percent
- FDA classification: Major drug interaction; prescriber alert required
- Evidence level: Well-established; multiple randomized controlled trials

## Fluconazole + Tacrolimus
- Interaction ID: DDI-002
- Drug A: Fluconazole
- Drug B: Tacrolimus
- Severity: Major (Contraindicated in many guidelines)
- Mechanism: Fluconazole inhibits CYP3A4, which extensively metabolizes tacrolimus
- Clinical consequence: Tacrolimus blood levels increase 2 to 4 fold; nephrotoxicity and neurotoxicity risk
- Nephrotoxicity presentation: Rising serum creatinine, reduced urine output
- Neurotoxicity presentation: Tremor, headache, confusion, seizures
- Rejection consequence: If tacrolimus falls below therapeutic range (opposite scenario with CYP3A4 inducers), rejection occurs
- Management: Mandatory tacrolimus level monitoring daily for first week; reduce tacrolimus dose 30 to 50 percent proactively; consider alternative antifungal (anidulafungin, micafungin) without CYP3A4 interaction
- Alternative antifungal recommendation: Echinocandins (anidulafungin, caspofungin, micafungin) not metabolized by CYP enzymes; preferred in transplant patients

## Fluconazole + Cyclosporine
- Interaction ID: DDI-003
- Drug A: Fluconazole
- Drug B: Cyclosporine
- Severity: Major
- Mechanism: Fluconazole inhibits CYP3A4, which metabolizes cyclosporine
- Clinical consequence: Cyclosporine levels increase 50 to 100 percent; nephrotoxicity
- Management: Monitor cyclosporine levels; reduce cyclosporine dose; consider echinocandin antifungal
- Evidence level: Well-established

## Fluconazole + Sulfonylureas (Glipizide, Glyburide, Glimepiride)
- Interaction ID: DDI-004
- Drug A: Fluconazole
- Drug B: Sulfonylureas (class interaction)
- Severity: Major
- Mechanism: Fluconazole inhibits CYP2C9, which metabolizes sulfonylureas; increased drug levels
- Clinical consequence: Prolonged and severe hypoglycemia
- Duration of risk: Persists for duration of fluconazole course plus 5 to 6 days after last dose (fluconazole half-life)
- Patient population at highest risk: Elderly, renal impairment, inadequate caloric intake
- Management: Blood glucose monitoring more frequently; counsel patient on hypoglycemia symptoms; consider sulfonylurea dose reduction; ensure patient not skipping meals
- Alternative: If antifungal treatment essential, consider non-CYP2C9-dependent antifungal

## Fluconazole + Phenytoin
- Interaction ID: DDI-005
- Drug A: Fluconazole
- Drug B: Phenytoin
- Severity: Major
- Mechanism: Fluconazole inhibits CYP2C9, which is primary enzyme metabolizing phenytoin; phenytoin also inhibits CYP2C9
- Clinical consequence: Phenytoin toxicity (nausea, vomiting, nystagmus, ataxia, confusion, seizures)
- Narrow therapeutic index note: Phenytoin therapeutic range 10 to 20 mg/L; small increases cause toxicity
- Management: Monitor phenytoin levels; reduce phenytoin dose; increased neurological monitoring

## Fluconazole + Simvastatin
- Interaction ID: DDI-006
- Drug A: Fluconazole
- Drug B: Simvastatin
- Severity: Moderate to Major
- Mechanism: Fluconazole inhibits CYP3A4 (moderate); simvastatin extensively metabolized by CYP3A4
- Clinical consequence: Simvastatin accumulation; myopathy, rhabdomyolysis risk
- Management: Consider temporary suspension of simvastatin during fluconazole course; monitor for muscle symptoms; use atorvastatin at low dose as alternative (less CYP3A4 dependent)

## Fluconazole + Amiodarone
- Interaction ID: DDI-007
- Drug A: Fluconazole
- Drug B: Amiodarone
- Severity: Major
- Mechanism: Both drugs inhibit CYP2C9; additive inhibition; both also prolong QT interval
- Dual risk: Pharmacokinetic (amiodarone levels affected) and pharmacodynamic (additive QT prolongation)
- QT prolongation consequence: Risk of torsades de pointes, a potentially fatal ventricular arrhythmia
- Management: Avoid combination if possible; if essential, ECG monitoring for QT interval; electrolyte monitoring (hypokalemia worsens QT risk)

## ACE Inhibitor + Potassium-Sparing Diuretic
- Interaction ID: DDI-008
- Drug A: ACE inhibitor class (Lisinopril, Enalapril, Ramipril)
- Drug B: Potassium-sparing diuretic class (Spironolactone, Eplerenone, Amiloride)
- Severity: Moderate to Major (risk increases with CKD)
- Mechanism: ACE inhibitors reduce aldosterone, retaining potassium; potassium-sparing diuretics also retain potassium; additive hyperkalemia
- Clinical consequence: Hyperkalemia; cardiac arrhythmia; cardiac arrest
- Risk amplification: CKD impairs potassium excretion; combination in CKD patient is high risk
- Monitoring: Serum potassium 1 week after starting combination, then every 3 months
- Threshold: If potassium above 5.5 mEq/L, reassess combination; above 6.0 mEq/L, hold one or both agents
- Note: In heart failure with reduced EF, combination is indicated and benefits outweigh risks with careful monitoring

## ACE Inhibitor + NSAIDs
- Interaction ID: DDI-009
- Drug A: ACE inhibitor class
- Drug B: NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen, indomethacin, etc.)
- Severity: Moderate
- Mechanism: NSAIDs inhibit prostaglandin synthesis; prostaglandins normally dilate afferent arteriole; NSAIDs cause afferent constriction, reducing GFR
- Clinical consequence: Acute kidney injury; reduced antihypertensive effect of ACE inhibitor; sodium retention
- Risk amplification: Existing CKD, dehydration, elderly patients
- Management: Avoid NSAID use in patients on ACE inhibitors when possible; use acetaminophen instead; if NSAID necessary, limit duration and monitor renal function

## Metformin + IV Contrast Media
- Interaction ID: DDI-010
- Drug A: Metformin
- Drug B: Iodinated intravenous contrast agents
- Severity: Major
- Mechanism: IV contrast can cause contrast-induced nephropathy; reduced renal function impairs metformin clearance; metformin accumulates; lactic acidosis risk
- Management: Hold Metformin day of procedure; hold for 48 hours after IV contrast; resume only after confirming stable renal function
- Risk stratification: Higher risk in patients with baseline GFR below 60, dehydration, multiple contrast doses

## Warfarin + Amiodarone
- Interaction ID: DDI-011
- Drug A: Warfarin
- Drug B: Amiodarone
- Severity: Major
- Mechanism: Amiodarone inhibits CYP2C9 (metabolizes S-warfarin) and CYP3A4 (metabolizes R-warfarin)
- Clinical consequence: Warfarin levels increase significantly; INR may increase 2 to 3 fold
- Onset: Gradual, over weeks; due to amiodarone's very long half-life (40 to 55 days)
- Duration after amiodarone stopped: Interaction may persist 3 to 6 months
- Management: Reduce warfarin dose 30 to 50 percent when starting amiodarone; intensive INR monitoring weekly initially

## Amiodarone + Digoxin
- Interaction ID: DDI-012
- Drug A: Amiodarone
- Drug B: Digoxin
- Severity: Major
- Mechanism: Amiodarone inhibits P-glycoprotein; P-gp normally pumps digoxin out of tissues; P-gp inhibition increases digoxin levels
- Expected digoxin level increase: 70 to 100 percent (levels may double)
- Clinical consequence: Digoxin toxicity (nausea, vomiting, visual disturbances, arrhythmias)
- Management: Reduce digoxin dose by 50 percent when starting amiodarone; monitor digoxin levels within 1 week

## Simvastatin + Clarithromycin
- Interaction ID: DDI-013
- Drug A: Simvastatin
- Drug B: Clarithromycin
- Severity: Contraindicated
- Mechanism: Clarithromycin is strong CYP3A4 inhibitor; simvastatin extensively metabolized by CYP3A4
- Clinical consequence: Simvastatin levels increase 10-fold; rhabdomyolysis risk
- Management: Do not prescribe together; if antibiotic required, use azithromycin (not CYP3A4 inhibitor) instead; if clarithromycin essential, suspend simvastatin during course plus 1 week after

## Clopidogrel + Omeprazole
- Interaction ID: DDI-014
- Drug A: Clopidogrel
- Drug B: Omeprazole
- Severity: Moderate to Major
- Mechanism: Omeprazole inhibits CYP2C19; clopidogrel requires CYP2C19 for activation to active metabolite
- Clinical consequence: Reduced clopidogrel antiplatelet effect; stent thrombosis risk
- FDA advisory: 2009 advisory against routine combination; use pantoprazole (minimal CYP2C19 inhibition) instead if PPI required

## Tacrolimus + Rifampin
- Interaction ID: DDI-015
- Drug A: Tacrolimus
- Drug B: Rifampin
- Severity: Major (Contraindicated without extreme caution)
- Mechanism: Rifampin is potent CYP3A4 inducer and P-gp inducer; massively increases tacrolimus metabolism
- Clinical consequence: Tacrolimus levels fall to subtherapeutic within days; organ rejection risk
- Management: Avoid combination if possible; if essential, may require 3 to 5 fold tacrolimus dose increase; daily level monitoring
